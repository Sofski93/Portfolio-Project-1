Pyramids

The three largest and best preserved pyramids are named after the pharaohs Cheops, Chefren and Menkaura, while the other three pyramids are nameless . Together with the Sphinx, which is also located in the area, the pyramids are some of the world's most famous structures. The terrain around the pyramids is flat. The highest point nearby is 500 meters above sea level, 1.0 km southwest of the pyramids. Around the Giza Plateau it is densely populated, with 369 inhabitants per square kilometer. Nearest major community is Cairo, The area is barren with little or no vegetation. A hot desert climate prevails in the area. The average annual temperature in the area is 25 °C. The warmest month is July, when the average temperature is 35 °C, and the coldest is January, with 14 °C. Average annual rainfall is 41 millimeters. The rainiest month is January, with an average of 14 mm of precipitation, and the driest is April, with 1 mm of precipitation.
